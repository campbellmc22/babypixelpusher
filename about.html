Frequently Asked Questions

Aren't you just another student from a UX Bootcamp?

Nope! My story starts five years ago with a project management software company (Project Insight). Like many women in the industry, I joined the company as a member of the Sales and Marketing team. I soon realized I had a knack for talking with customers about their interface experience. This grew into performing the User Experience role at Project Insight. It was only after I discovered I only knew a small sliver of the UX field, that I enrolled in the University of California, Irvine's UX/UI bootcamp.


So what am I going to see in your portfolio? A bunch of student work?

Some. Some student work because I worked hard to learn the basics of the design craft through my assigned homework. You'll also see work from Project Insight where I designed for the webapp, as well as, the website and our Zendesk Help Center. I do it all right now at my current company, but I want to focus into the area of User Research and User Interaction Design. It's time to narrow my focus.


How did you give your best effort for 16 weekly hours of class for three nights a week (plus 20-30 hours a week out of class for assignments) AND work full-time?

I kicked my own bootie, that's how! I value work/life balance above all else, and the technology field, as a whole, is equally passionate about finding that balance in creative ways. I did sacrifice many Friday and Saturday nights for studying/working hard. I set up study sessions with other budding designers in my class and was lovingly called "mom" for keeping us on task. I find when you're passionate about what you are doing you find time to make it happen. When you aren't passionate, you'll find excuses to not accomplish your goals.
